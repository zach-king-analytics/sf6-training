# Mental Resilience

**Definition:** The ability to maintain deliberate play and protocol compliance under pressure — especially after a bad round, a loss streak, or a frustrating matchup interaction — without drifting into autopilot or tilt-queueing.

## Recognition Cues

- You re-queued immediately after a loss without writing the loss entry
- You abandoned the session focus after one bad round
- You tried a different game plan mid-match with no evidence for it
- You noticed frustration before a match and queued anyway

## Tilt Signals (Personal)

| Signal | What it looks like |
|--------|--------------------|
| Early | Pressing more buttons than usual; skipping spacing checks |
| Mid | Ignoring session focus; switching strategies without reason |
| Late | Re-queueing without loss review; playing past planned session end |

## Reset Protocol

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| After any ranked loss | Write loss entry before re-queuing |
| Two losses in a row | Read session intent section before next match |
| Tilt signal detected | 5-minute break; re-read gameplan Non-Negotiables |
| Session end | Run session_close.py — no exceptions |

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Cause | Correction |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Tilt-queueing | Emotional re-queue | Loss entry is the gate, not a suggestion |
| Plan switching | Frustration, not evidence | Only change game plan based on replay data |
| Autopilot | Low energy or high frustration | Lower session length; not session intensity |
