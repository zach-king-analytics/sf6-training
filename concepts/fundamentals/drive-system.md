# Drive System

**Definition:** The six-bar resource meter governing Drive Rush, Drive Impact, Drive Parry, and Overdrive. Managing gauge determines what offensive and defensive options are available at any moment in a round.

---

| Field | Value |

## Recognition Cues

- You entered burnout without a clear reason
- You used DR in a situation where advantage was not confirmed
- You panic-used DI in neutral rather than as a read
- You had < 2 bars when a punish opportunity appeared

## Core Mechanics

| Mechanic | Gauge Cost | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Drive Rush (cancel) | 3 bars | High cost; confirm advantage first |
| Drive Rush (dash) | 1.5 bars | Lower cost; good for positioning |
| Drive Impact | 1 bar | Read-based; not reactive panic |
| Drive Parry (hold) | Drains slowly | Drain rate faster in burnout |
| Overdrive (EX) | 2 bars | Budget against Drive Rush spend |

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Cause | Correction |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Burnout early round | Reckless DR usage | Track DR spend; DR only on confirmed advantage |
| DI as panic button | Fear-based | DI is a read; if unsure, do not press |
| No gauge for punish | Poor budgeting | Mental note of gauge before each neutral exchange |
| Burned out vs. burnout opp | Double burnout | Preserve at least 2 bars when opponent is near burnout |
